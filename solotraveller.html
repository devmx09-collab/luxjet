6 Ways Solo Travelers Fly Private for Less

1) Empty‑leg flights

Significantly discounted one‑way segments when operators reposition aircraft. Availability is limited and dates are fixed, but savings can be 30–70%.

Browse empty-leg offers

2) Seat-share / individual seats

Some operators sell individual seats on larger cabins—ideal for cost-conscious solo travelers who want private benefits without the full charter price.

Check seat-share availability

3) Split-charter or empty-seat pricing

Split costs on mid-size or larger jets for common routes; good for business travelers splitting trips or returning the same day.

Request a split-seat quote

4) Be flexible with airports & timings

Flying mid-week, accepting nearby airports, or flexible departure times increases cheap positioning options and empty‑leg matches.

5) Last-minute deals

Operators often discount flights to avoid repositioning costs—subscribe to alerts on booking platforms for immediate offers.

6) Choose smaller or one-way positioning legs

Very-light and light jets cost less per-hour for short hops; positioning-only routes can be heavily discounted.

Quick Comparison

  • Empty leg: High discount, fixed schedule.
  • Seat-share: Seat-level booking, limited routes.
  • Split-charter: Flexible, needs matching travelers.
  • Last‑minute: Opportunistic savings.

Solo Traveler Tips

  • Pack light to fit smaller-cabin limits.
  • Request a la carte catering to avoid extras.
  • Use landing/terminal proximity to save ground time and cost.
  • Be ready to confirm quickly—deals disappear fast.
