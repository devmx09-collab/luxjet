How booking works

Start with dates, passenger count, luggage and preferred airports. Brokers often provide instant preliminary quotes; select an option, sign the trip sheet or contract, and pay a deposit or full amount. Confirm manifests and special requests 24–72 hours before departure.

What flying private is like

Faster, more flexible, and more comfortable than commercial travel: skip long lines, use smaller airports, and enjoy personalized onboard service and faster door‑to-door time.

Pricing & instant quotes

Instant quotes combine hourly rates, estimated block time, and known fees (FBO handling, taxes, positioning). Always request an itemized breakdown showing flight time, repositioning, handling, catering, and surcharges.

Quick tips for first timers

  • Check baggage rules for your aircraft class.
  • Share dietary, pet, mobility, and pickup details early.
  • Request operator credentials (AOC, insurance, audits) if you need reassurance.
  • Be punctual; follow the operator's arrival window.

FAQs (first-time private flyers)

Common questions: booking lead times, baggage limits, pet policies, accessible travel, payment/cancellation terms, and how quotes are calculated.

Booking scenarios

Short business hops: light jets. Coast‑to‑coast or international nonstop: super‑midsize or heavy jets. Book peak dates early and be flexible on times to save.

Typical booking procedure

  1. Request or search with dates, pax, luggage, airports.
  2. Receive quotes and aircraft options.
  3. Select aircraft and confirm price.
  4. Sign contract and pay deposit/full amount.
  5. Prepare manifest, catering, ground transport.
  6. Flight day: FBO access and expedited boarding.
