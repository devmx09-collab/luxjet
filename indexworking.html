Private Jet Pricing Explained

Private jet charter pricing varies depending on aircraft size, routing, airport fees, positioning costs, fuel prices, and trip duration. Instant quotes provide transparency so you can compare aircraft and operators.

Typical quote components include flight hours, crew expenses, airport handling fees, taxes, catering, and repositioning flights when required.

First-Time Private Jet Flyer Guide

If you've never flown privately before, the experience is significantly different from commercial air travel.

Before Departure

  • Submit passenger information and identification.
  • Confirm baggage requirements.
  • Request catering or special accommodations.
  • Arrange ground transportation if required.

At the Private Terminal

Most flights depart from Fixed Base Operators (FBOs), private terminals that offer expedited check-in, comfortable lounges, and direct aircraft access.

Boarding

Boarding typically begins shortly before departure. There are no long security lines, crowded gates, or boarding groups.

Arrival

Private aircraft often use smaller airports closer to your final destination, reducing overall travel time.

Empty Leg Flights

Empty leg flights occur when an aircraft repositions without passengers. These flights can offer substantial savings compared to standard charter pricing, although schedules and availability are limited.
